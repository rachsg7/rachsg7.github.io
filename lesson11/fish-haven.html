Creating a warm, cozy glow with lighting

by Nancy A. Herrick

Country Living magazine called the little cottage the "coziest house ever."

With its angled ceilings, rich wood accents and giant stone fireplace, it certainly would qualify for that honor. But it's the lighting that makes the small house in Montana particularly warm and inviting.

A quaint chandelier illuminates the small dining table. Sconces cast a mellow glow on either side of the bed, and a candle flickers on a tabletop near the fireplace. An overhead fixture with just six candelabra bulbs provides adequate light, and plenty of atmosphere, in the living area.

"A space that's cozy often is simple, romantic," says Candace Miller of Miller Architects, based in Montana, who helped design the space. "The lighting adds to the warmth. It just glows."
